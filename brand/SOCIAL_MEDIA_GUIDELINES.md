## Platform Strategy

### Our Social Philosophy

Lazy's social presence should feel like the protocol itself: **minimal effort to engage with, maximum value delivered.**

We don't chase engagement through controversy or hype. We build trust through consistency, honesty, and quiet confidence. Our social accounts should make followers feel smarter, not anxious.

### Platform Priorities

| Platform | Priority | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Twitter/X** | Primary | Announcements, community, thought leadership |
| **Discord** | Primary | Support, community, governance |
| **Farcaster** | Secondary | Crypto-native audience, experimentation |
| **Telegram** | Secondary | Announcements only (no active community) |
| **LinkedIn** | Tertiary | Institutional credibility, hiring |

### Twitter Don'ts

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Use "gm/gn" unironically | Signals we're trying too hard to fit in |
| Promise specific APYs | Yields change; never guarantee numbers |
| Dunk on competitors by name | Looks insecure; let the product speak |
| Use rocket/moon emojis | Memecoin energy we're avoiding |
| Respond to every FUD | Pick battles; silence is often best |
| Announce partnerships before they're real | Erodes trust |
| Use "SOON" or "announcement coming" | Either announce or don't |
| Thread everything | Most ideas don't need 12 tweets |

### Twitter Do's

| Do | Why |
|----|-----|
| Respond to genuine questions quickly | Builds trust and community |
| Admit when something goes wrong | Honesty > spin |
| Share the "why" behind decisions | Transparency builds loyalty |
| Amplify community members | They're the real marketing |
| Keep most tweets under 200 characters | Respect attention spans |
| Use visuals for data | Charts > walls of text |
| Have a human sign off on tone | Avoid robotic corporate voice |

- Patient and thorough
- Never condescending

- More formal, structured
- Focus on facts and process
- Encourage participation

```

Cons: Slightly higher operational overhead

Discussion period: 7 days
Vote: [link]

Share your thoughts below.
```

### Discord Don'ts

- Don't ignore questions (even if asked beforeâ€”link to answers)
- Don't argue with trolls (one response max, then ignore)
- Don't make promises you can't keep
- Don't share alpha or speculation about unreleased features
- Don't use @everyone unless absolutely necessary

### Moderation Tone

When moderating, be firm but not aggressive:

```
Good: "This channel is for support questions. For price discussion, please use #general."

Bad: "READ THE RULES. This is your only warning."
```

## Crisis Communication

### Principles

1. **Speed over polish** â€” Acknowledge fast, details follow
2. **Honesty over spin** â€” Never downplay or hide
3. **Responsibility over blame** â€” Own mistakes fully
4. **Action over words** â€” Say what you're doing, then do it

### Response Templates

#### Security Incident (Minor)

```
We identified an issue with [X].

What happened: [brief explanation]
Impact: [who/what was affected]
Status: [resolved/investigating]

No funds are at risk. Full post-mortem coming within 24 hours.

Updates in this thread.
```

#### Security Incident (Major)

```
We are investigating a security incident affecting [X].

Deposits and withdrawals are paused while we assess.

If you have funds in Lazy, do not interact with any contracts until we confirm they are safe.

We will share everything we know as soon as we know it.
```

#### Delayed Feature/Withdrawal

```
Withdrawals are taking longer than usual.

Why: [honest reason]
Expected resolution: [time]

Your funds are safe. We're working to process the backlog.

Sorry for the waitâ€”we'll update every [X] hours until resolved.
```

### What NOT to Do in a Crisis

- Don't delete tweets/messages (archive is forever)
- Don't blame external factors without owning your part
- Don't go silent (even "we're investigating" is better)
- Don't promise timelines you can't keep
- Don't use humor to deflect

## Metrics That Matter

### Vanity Metrics (Track But Don't Optimize For)

- Follower count
- Like count
- Impressions

### Meaningful Metrics

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Reply rate | Are people engaging, not just scrolling? |
| Save/bookmark rate | Is content valuable enough to return to? |
| Click-through to product | Does social drive action? |
| Support ticket volume | Is proactive communication reducing confusion? |
| Sentiment in replies | Quality of engagement > quantity |
| Time to response (support) | Are we actually helping? |

### Red Flags to Watch

- Sudden spike in negative replies
- Repeated questions about the same issue
- Declining engagement on announcements
- Community members correcting misinformation

## Team Voice vs. Brand Voice

### Official @lazy Account

- Represents the protocol
- Slightly more formal
- No personal opinions on politics/unrelated topics
- Consistent across all team members posting

### Team Member Accounts

Team members can have personal voices, but when discussing Lazy:

- Make clear you work at Lazy
- Don't leak unreleased information
- Don't make promises on behalf of the protocol
- Don't argue with critics (tag official account if needed)

### Founder Voice

Founders can be more personal and opinionated, but:

- Separate personal takes from protocol positions
- Avoid using founder status to "win" arguments
- Be accessible but maintain boundaries

## Quick Reference: Voice Checklist

Before posting, ask:

- [ ] Would a smart friend talk like this?
- [ ] Is this honest? (Would we stand by it if screenshotted?)
- [ ] Is this necessary? (Does it add value?)
- [ ] Is this clear? (Would a newcomer understand?)
- [ ] Is this on-brand? (Calm, confident, helpful?)
- [ ] Could this age poorly? (Promises, predictions, takes?)

If any answer is "no," revise or don't post.
